  In the past, teachers have been told to avoid speaking about controversial topics in schools. Some education guides have advised to avoid speaking about it. However, book banning is much more recent in American history. In an article by the Washington Post called "Todayâ€™s book bans might be more dangerous than those from the past" by Jonna Perillo, she talks aobut how there was some book banning occuring in the McCarthy era and also the early 1980's. However, these earlier book bans focused more on books that students would most definetely have to read, like textbooks and course content. They also targeted content that went against mainstream ideas of capitalism, or in the south, went against segregation. Textbooks primarily portrayed white children, although it wasn't due to an explicit ban, rather an unsaid rule. However, book bans today aim books that children may optionally read. Rather than allowing a wide range of books to choose from for personal reading, they have limited choice reading, a thing that didn't happen previously. During the McCarthy era, book bans were not just in schools, but proponents sought to also get them removed from bookstores and such. However, in this era of book banning, books are banned only in the schools, and not outside. On top of that, these books are not books that are taught in classes.
