Seeing this change in the limitations we have set for our children, we can see that book bans are going to be a big part of the issues concerning education in future years. But first, let us see the current policies we have made surrounding book bans.

  In North Carolina, the issue of book bans falls under broader laws governing educational materials and public education policies. The authority to determine which books are included or excluded from school libraries often lies with local school boards, guided by guidelines set forth under statutes such as Chapter 115C-Elementary and Secondary Education. To be more specific, section 115C-98 deals with the selection and provision of textbooks and supplementary materials. However, it seems that book bans are becoming more serious, especially on books on race, sexuality, and gender identity.
